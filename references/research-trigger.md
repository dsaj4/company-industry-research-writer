# Research Trigger

Use this reference to decide when the report needs deeper research before drafting.

## Principle

Do not browse or collect sources just because research feels useful. Trigger deeper research when it changes factual reliability, thesis quality, or valuation discipline.

## Trigger conditions

Run a deeper research pass if any of these are true:

### 1. The report depends on unstable facts

Examples:

- product launches
- pricing updates
- traffic data
- policy changes
- financing round details
- model rankings
- market-share or usage shifts

### 2. The report needs multi-source confirmation

Examples:

- private-company valuation claims
- revenue estimates or revenue clues
- competitive position claims
- third-party benchmark interpretation
- disputed market narratives

### 3. The report includes high-stakes analytical language

Examples:

- strong differentiation claims
- commercialization claims
- valuation framing
- category leadership claims

### 4. The available materials are obviously incomplete

Examples:

- only one side of the story is present
- no official source exists in the current pack
- screenshots exist without context
- the draft contains many "probably" claims with no sourcing trail

## When not to do a large research pass

Do not run a full research loop when:

- the task is only chapter compression or style cleanup
- the report is using a fixed offline source pack and the user does not want fresh web verification
- the current issue is structure, not facts

In these cases, do targeted source checks only.

## Recommended source mix

For company and industry reports, prefer this order:

1. official company materials
2. filings, audited statements, or regulated disclosures
3. reputable third-party data platforms
4. reputable media or financing coverage
5. ecosystem signals such as GitHub, pricing pages, traffic tools, and benchmark sites

## Research output contract

When a deep research pass is triggered, save `01_research.md` and include:

- key questions being checked
- source list with links or file paths
- confirmed facts
- unresolved conflicts
- informed inferences
- facts that must be downgraded in wording

## Red-flag topics that require extra caution

- private-company valuation
- revenue claims without audited backing
- chart screenshots with unclear date ranges
- benchmark claims built on only one ranking or one article
- "market leader" or "best-in-class" language

## Drafting rule after research

After research, convert every important claim into one of:

- confirmed fact
- informed inference
- working hypothesis

Do not take raw research notes directly into polished prose without confidence labeling.

# Outline

## Report structure observed in the sample

1. Title page with core conclusions and financial snapshot
2. Table of contents
3. Figure and table index
4. Company overview
5. Industry analysis
6. Company highlights
7. Earnings forecast and investment recommendation
8. Risk factors
9. Financial statements and disclosures

## Chapter plan abstraction

### Front page

- Judgment:
  - the sample states the thesis before the body starts
- Question answered:
  - why the company matters
- Expected evidence:
  - summary bullets
  - forecast snapshot
  - rating block

### Company overview

- Judgment:
  - who the company is and what stage it has reached
- Question answered:
  - what has the company built and how is it organized
- Expected evidence:
  - timeline
  - management table
  - product matrix
  - pricing information

### Industry analysis

- Judgment:
  - why the market and technology backdrop justify attention
- Question answered:
  - is the sector attractive and how is competition evolving
- Expected evidence:
  - technology timeline
  - cost-down trends
  - market-size and peer charts

### Company highlights

- Judgment:
  - why this company stands out versus peers
- Question answered:
  - what combination of model, product, and commercialization makes it special
- Expected evidence:
  - model ranking charts
  - product revenue split
  - usage and paying-customer metrics
  - global expansion data

### Forecast and valuation

- Judgment:
  - how the report converts the thesis into revenue and valuation logic
- Question answered:
  - what economic path justifies the recommendation
- Expected evidence:
  - business-split forecast table
  - comparable-company valuation table

### Risks

- Judgment:
  - what can break the thesis
- Question answered:
  - where execution, competition, and IP risk remain
- Expected evidence:
  - explicit risk list with short explanations

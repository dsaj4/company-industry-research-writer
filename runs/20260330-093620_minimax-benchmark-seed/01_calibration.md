# Calibration

## Five things to imitate

1. High-density opening page that states core judgments before details.
2. Stable chapter order: company overview -> industry analysis -> company highlights -> valuation -> risks.
3. Figure and table planning with clear evidence responsibilities.
4. Judgment-first section writing, where each chapter answers a distinct question.
5. A closing sequence in which valuation and risks gather and test the thesis.

## Five things that must remain different

1. Do not inherit listed-equity rating language when the target report is not a stock call.
2. Do not inherit MiniMax-specific facts, business splits, product matrix, or forecasts.
3. Do not force private-company work into a public-company financial-statement template.
4. Do not reuse target-price or comparable-PS logic without re-grounding to the new subject.
5. Do not let the benchmark create a shell-copy report that looks like the sample with renamed entities.

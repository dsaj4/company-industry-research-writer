# Outline

## Report structure observed in the sample

1. Title page with core conclusions and financial snapshot
2. Table of contents
3. Figure and table index
4. Investment highlights page
5. Company overview
6. Industry analysis
7. Analogue-comparison chapter
8. Challenge or constraint chapter
9. Earnings forecast and investment recommendation
10. Risk factors
11. Financial statements and disclosures

## Chapter plan abstraction

### Front matter

- Judgment:
  - the sample defines the thesis and key support points before the main body
- Question answered:
  - why this company deserves deep-dive attention
- Expected evidence:
  - summary bullets
  - financial snapshot
  - rating block

### Company overview

- Judgment:
  - the company is a platform-centered enterprise AI vendor with expanding industry coverage
- Question answered:
  - what the company sells, how it is architected, and how it reaches customers
- Expected evidence:
  - product architecture
  - customer strategy
  - shareholding
  - timeline

### Industry analysis

- Judgment:
  - decision intelligence is a real generative-AI application layer, not just a generic software label
- Question answered:
  - why this application category matters now
- Expected evidence:
  - market-structure charts
  - product-standardization arguments
  - category-definition visuals

### Analogue-comparison chapter

- Judgment:
  - the company's positioning becomes clearer when contrasted with an overseas reference point
- Question answered:
  - where the business resembles and differs from a known analogue
- Expected evidence:
  - external analogue product and financial charts
  - product-matrix comparison
  - customer comparison

### Challenge chapter

- Judgment:
  - commercialization still faces meaningful constraints
- Question answered:
  - what could limit the strategic story before valuation even begins
- Expected evidence:
  - payment willingness
  - sales expense
  - receivables
  - data quality and security issues

### Forecast and valuation

- Judgment:
  - the report turns segment-level assumptions into valuation logic
- Question answered:
  - what operating path supports the recommendation
- Expected evidence:
  - split forecast table
  - comparables

### Risks

- Judgment:
  - formal downside cases remain even after the challenge chapter
- Question answered:
  - what can still derail the thesis
- Expected evidence:
  - concise risk list with explanation

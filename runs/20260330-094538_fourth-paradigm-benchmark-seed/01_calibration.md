# Calibration

## Five things to imitate

1. High-density front page plus a dedicated investment-highlights layer.
2. Strong figure and table planning before the body starts.
3. Enterprise-platform framing in company overview, including product architecture and customer strategy.
4. Use of a comparison chapter when an external analogue materially sharpens positioning.
5. Willingness to surface challenge and constraint sections before valuation instead of hiding all uncertainty in the final risk page.

## Five things that must remain different

1. Do not inherit listed-equity rating language for non-equity outputs.
2. Do not force a Palantir-style analogue chapter unless a real analogue exists.
3. Do not copy Fourth Paradigm's business split, industry coverage, or forecast assumptions.
4. Do not treat every enterprise-AI report as if it needs the same challenge chapter structure.
5. Do not let platform-oriented benchmark logic erase the distinct requirements of model-company or private-company research.

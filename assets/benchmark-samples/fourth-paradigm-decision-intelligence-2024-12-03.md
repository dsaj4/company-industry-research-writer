## Key chapter logic

### Company overview

Role:

- define the product architecture
- explain platform-centered business structure
- show industry penetration and customer strategy

### Industry analysis

Role:

- argue that decision intelligence is a meaningful generative-AI application layer
- explain why enterprise software can be reshaped by generative AI

### Comparative analysis chapter

Role:

- use a strong external analogue to sharpen the company's positioning
- show where the company resembles and differs from a leading overseas reference point

This is a useful variant chapter type when a direct competitor or analogue can clarify the thesis.

### Challenge chapter

Role:

- surface unresolved issues before formal valuation
- make the report feel less promotional

This is another useful variant: some reports benefit from a dedicated "challenge/constraint" chapter before the final recommendation.

### Forecast and recommendation

Role:

- translate the strategic thesis into segment-level forecast and valuation logic

### Risks

## What to imitate

1. Use a platform-and-solution architecture lens when the company is enterprise-first.
2. Allow a dedicated comparison chapter when a strong external analogue sharpens the thesis.
3. Treat "challenge/constraint" as a legitimate pre-valuation chapter when uncertainty is structurally important.
4. Combine product architecture, customer strategy, and industry penetration into the company-overview evidence burden.
5. Use a business-split forecast table only when the company type and evidence quality support it.

## What not to copy

1. Do not inherit stock-rating language for non-equity outputs.
2. Do not import its listed-company valuation logic into private-company work without re-grounding.
3. Do not force a Palantir-style analogy where no real analogue exists.
4. Do not copy its exact business segmentation, industry coverage, or forecast assumptions.
5. Do not let "challenge chapter" become a generic dumping ground instead of a thesis-testing section.

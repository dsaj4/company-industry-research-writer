## Evidence pattern

The report uses at least five evidence classes:

1. company timeline and management tables
2. product screenshots and pricing tables
3. financial charts and business-split charts
4. third-party market and benchmark charts
5. comparable-company valuation table

This is one reason it works well as a benchmark sample: charts are not decorative. They do chapter work.

## What to imitate

1. Start with a high-density front page that states the core judgment early.
2. Keep the chapter order stable: company, industry, highlights, valuation, risks.
3. Use figure and table indexes to enforce evidence planning, even if the final report does not expose them separately.
4. Make each chapter answer a clear question and carry its own evidence burden.
5. Let the valuation and risk sections gather and test the entire thesis.

## What not to copy

1. Do not inherit stock-rating language when the target is not a listed equity report.
2. Do not inherit target-price or PS multiple logic for private-company work without re-grounding.
3. Do not copy the sample company's business split, user metrics, or financial assumptions.
4. Do not import public-equity financial statement machinery into a private-company report by default.
5. Do not let the benchmark force the new report into a shell copy.

# AI Company Benchmark Cluster V1

This note synthesizes the first two seeded benchmark samples in the workspace:

- `minimax-born-global-2026-02-10.md`
- `fourth-paradigm-decision-intelligence-2024-12-03.md`

## Why this cluster matters

One benchmark can teach shape, but it can also bias the skill toward one company archetype.

These two samples together are more useful because they cover two different AI-company report types:

1. model-and-product-led AI company
2. enterprise-platform and decision-intelligence AI company

The shared rules between them are strong candidates for core skill behavior.

The differences between them are strong candidates for variant selection logic.

## Shared structure across both benchmarks

Both samples reinforce the following common spine:

1. high-density front page with core judgments
2. explicit chapter order rather than material-dump order
3. company overview
4. industry analysis
5. company-specific differentiation chapter
6. financial, commercialization, or valuation discussion
7. risks

Both also treat:

- figure and table planning as part of the argument
- valuation and risk as thesis-gathering sections, not afterthoughts
- benchmark-quality writing as judgment-first and evidence-backed

## Shared non-transferables across both benchmarks

Even though both are useful, neither should donate these by default:

- stock rating language
- target price logic
- public-equity recommendation framing
- company-specific forecasts
- company-specific business splits
- listed-company financial-statement machinery for private-company work

Current sample:

## What the cluster teaches

### 1. The front matter should be heavy

Both samples prove that a serious benchmark does not hide the thesis until page 5.

### 2. Charts need jobs

Both samples are chart-dense, but the density works because the charts answer chapter questions.

### 3. AI reports need an economic bridge

Neither sample stops at technical capability.

They both connect:

- capability
- product or platform form
- adoption or customer logic
- commercial or economic implication

### 4. Variant chapters are legitimate

Not every report needs the same chapter set.

Examples from the cluster:

- comparison-led chapter
- challenge or constraint chapter
- stronger company-highlights chapter

### 5. The benchmark should fit the company archetype

The skill should choose the benchmark that matches the target company's shape, not just the report that looks best in isolation.

## Combination rule

When both benchmarks are available:

- choose one primary benchmark based on company archetype
- use the other only as a secondary pattern source
- do not merge every section from both into one bloated skeleton

## Current primary-selection heuristic

- If the target is model-led, consumer-led, or global-product-led:
  - start from `MINIMAX`
- If the target is enterprise-platform-led, customer-strategy-led, or solution-architecture-led:
  - start from `Fourth Paradigm`

## Gaps in the cluster

The current benchmark set still lacks:

- a private-company formal deep dive
- a non-equity industry-only report
- a high-quality SaaS-style benchmark without heavy model-company framing

## Immediate implications for the skill

1. Add benchmark-archetype selection logic to `references/`
2. Add evals that test whether the skill chooses the right benchmark family
3. Avoid hard-coding one benchmark's optional chapter variants into every report

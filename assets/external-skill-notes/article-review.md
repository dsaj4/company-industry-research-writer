# Notes From `article-review`

Source:

- https://raw.githubusercontent.com/zephyrwang6/myskill/main/article-review/SKILL.md

## Why it matters

This skill is not a formal research-report template, but it contains useful micro-patterns for making dense material more legible and memorable.

## Best patterns to borrow

### 1. Find the visible hook, then the real value

It distinguishes between:

- what gets attention first
- what the material is actually worth understanding

Research adaptation:

- use this in report intros and executive summaries
- separate the market headline from the deeper analytical takeaway

### 2. Extract the central concept and translate it

This is valuable when a report uses technical, financial, or industry terms that would otherwise become friction.

Research adaptation:

- keep the precise term
- add one plain-language explanation
- give one concrete implication

### 3. Quote or surface the strongest line, then interpret it

Useful for:

- founder interviews
- company positioning statements
- policy or analyst quotes

Research adaptation:

- treat the quote as evidence, then explain what it means for the thesis

### 4. Concluding lift

It ends by turning a dense reading into a clear takeaway.

Research adaptation:

- useful for end-of-chapter implications and executive summary close

## What not to borrow

- social spread or传播 framing as the main driver
- personality-heavy commentary as a substitute for research judgment

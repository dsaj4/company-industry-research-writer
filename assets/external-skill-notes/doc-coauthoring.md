# Notes From `doc-coauthoring`

Source:

- https://raw.githubusercontent.com/zephyrwang6/myskill/main/doc-coauthoring/SKILL.md

## Why it matters

This skill is small, but it has a good section-level iteration rhythm.

## Best patterns to borrow

### 1. Three-stage shape

- context gathering
- refinement and structure
- reader testing

Research adaptation:

- task intake
- thesis and chapter shaping
- quality review and reader-friction check

### 2. Section-by-section iteration

Its section loop is:

- brainstorm possible points
- choose what to keep
- write
- iterate fast

Research adaptation:

- subchapter breakdown
- decide evidence-backed claims to keep
- draft from zero
- compare against old draft and benchmark needs
- revise

### 3. Style preference capture

It captures style preferences early instead of fixing them late.

Research adaptation:

- capture report register early: formal or semi-formal, investor-facing or internal, concise or full-length

### 4. Reader testing

This is especially useful for checking where a dense chapter becomes confusing.

Research adaptation:

- use a short pass that predicts likely reader questions or doubts after each major chapter

## What not to borrow

- constant user micro-approvals for every section
- overuse of conversational writing anti-patterns that are less relevant to formal research prose

## Concrete future upgrades inspired by this skill

1. add a lightweight `reader-friction` checklist to chapter review
2. strengthen chapter rewrite mode in `references/iteration-and-orchestration.md`
3. add report register options to a future input contract
